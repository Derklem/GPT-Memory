**assistant:**

The **Affidavit of Support (Form I-864)** is a legally binding document that U.S. citizens or lawful permanent residents (LPRs) sign when sponsoring an immigrant for a green card. It serves as a contract between the sponsor, the sponsored immigrant, and the U.S. government to ensure that the immigrant does not become a public charge (i.e., reliant on certain government benefits).

### Key Points About the I-864 Obligation:
1. **Financial Responsibility**:
   - By signing Form I-864, the sponsor agrees to financially support the immigrant so that their income is at least **125% of the federal poverty level** for their household size.  
   - This obligation continues regardless of changes in the sponsor’s relationship with the immigrant (e.g., divorce).

2. **Duration of Obligation**:
   The sponsor's responsibility under the I-864 lasts until one of the following occurs:
   - The sponsored immigrant becomes a U.S. citizen.
   - The sponsored immigrant earns **40 qualifying quarters of work** (equivalent to approximately 10 years of work under Social Security rules).
   - The sponsored immigrant permanently departs the U.S.
   - The sponsored immigrant dies.
   - The sponsor dies (this terminates their obligation, but not any liabilities incurred while alive).

3. **Enforcement of the Obligation**:
   - The immigrant can legally enforce the sponsor’s financial support obligation through lawsuits if the sponsor fails to meet the income support requirement.
   - Certain government agencies may also seek reimbursement from the sponsor if the immigrant uses **means-tested public benefits** (e.g., food stamps, Medicaid).

4. **Public Benefits Considerations**:
   - If the sponsored immigrant applies for and receives specific means-tested benefits, the sponsor may be required to repay the government for those benefits.
   - Benefits like emergency medical assistance, school lunches, and disaster relief are generally exempt.

5. **Divorce and the I-864**:
   - Divorce does not end the I-864 obligation.
   - However, the sponsored immigrant’s ability to enforce the financial support may be impacted depending on the court's interpretation and jurisdiction.

6. **Co-sponsors**:
   - If there was a co-sponsor involved (someone who also signed Form I-864), they share the same obligations as the primary sponsor.
