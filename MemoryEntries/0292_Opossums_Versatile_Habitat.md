Opossums are quite adaptable and can be found in a variety of habitats, including forests, woodlands, farmlands, and urban areas. They often seek shelter in hollow trees, brush piles, and burrows dug by other animals. They are also known to inhabit areas near water sources like streams and rivers.

**assistant:**

To promote opossum habitat in suburban Southern California, consider planting native vegetation that provides food, shelter, and nesting sites for them. Here are some suggestions:

1. **Native Trees and Shrubs**: Plant native trees and shrubs such as oak trees, sycamores, willows, and California lilacs. These provide food in the form of fruits, nuts, and leaves, as well as shelter in their branches and foliage.

2. **Brush Piles**: Create brush piles using fallen branches, twigs, and leaves. These provide shelter and nesting sites for opossums, as well as habitat for the insects and other invertebrates they feed on.

3. **Ground Cover**: Plant native ground cover plants like ferns, native grasses, and wildflowers. These provide foraging opportunities for opossums and other small mammals while also stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

4. **Water Sources**: Include a small pond or shallow water feature if possible, as opossums need access to water for drinking and bathing.

5. **Rock Piles and Logs**: Incorporate rock piles and fallen logs into the landscape to create additional hiding spots and habitat diversity.

6. **Avoid Chemicals**: Minimize the use of pesticides and herbicides in your landscaping to ensure a healthy environment for opossums and other wildlife.

7. **Secure Nesting Sites**: Leave undisturbed areas with dense vegetation or create artificial nesting boxes in trees to provide safe places for opossums to raise their young.

By incorporating these elements into your suburban landscape, you can help enhance opossum habitat and support their population in Southern California.
