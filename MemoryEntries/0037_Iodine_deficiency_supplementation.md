You’re holding a container of **non-iodized salt**. Unlike iodized salt, this type does not provide iodine, an essential nutrient that supports thyroid function. If you use this salt exclusively, you’ll need to get iodine from other sources.

### Ways to Supplement Iodine:

**1. Dietary Sources**
   - **Seafood**: Fish (especially cod, tuna), seaweed (kelp, nori, wakame)
   - **Dairy**: Milk, yogurt, cheese
   - **Eggs**: Especially the yolk
   - **Fortified foods**: Some breads or cereals are fortified with iodine

**2. Iodized Salt**
   - Alternate between iodized and non-iodized salt in your cooking.

**3. Supplements**
   - **Iodine tablets or multivitamins** (only if recommended by a healthcare professional—too much iodine can be harmful).

### Important Note
- Most people in developed countries meet their iodine needs through a mix of food and iodized salt.
- **If you avoid all iodized salt and dairy/seafood, you should consider testing your iodine levels with a doctor.**
