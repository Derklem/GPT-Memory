Before beginning their Mars probe missions, the Soviet Union conducted a series of **Luna missions**, achieving several significant milestones. The **Luna program** started in 1959 and continued until the 1970s. Here's an overview of their successes before transitioning to Mars missions:

### **Notable Successful Luna Missions (Pre-Mars Program)**  
1. **Luna 1 (1959)**: The first spacecraft to escape Earth's gravity, though it missed the Moon and became the first artificial object in heliocentric orbit.  
2. **Luna 2 (1959)**: The first spacecraft to impact the Moon's surface intentionally.  
3. **Luna 3 (1959)**: The first mission to photograph the far side of the Moon.  
4. **Luna 9 (1966)**: The first successful soft landing on the Moon, sending panoramic images.  
5. **Luna 10 (1966)**: The first spacecraft to orbit the Moon.  
6. **Luna 16 (1970)**: The first robotic mission to return lunar soil samples to Earth.  
7. **Luna 17 (1970)**: Delivered the **Lunokhod 1**, the first robotic rover on the Moon.  
8. **Luna 20 (1972)**: Another successful soil sample return mission.  

### **Luna Successes Before Mars 3 (1971)**  
By the time the Soviets launched **Mars 3** in 1971, they had already completed **16 Luna missions** (excluding failures), covering impact missions, photography, orbiters, soft landings, sample returns, and rover deployments.

These successes with the Moon program helped lay the groundwork for the development of their Mars probes. However, the Mars missions were much more complex due to the greater distance, harsher conditions, and limited technology of the time.
