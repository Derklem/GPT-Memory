The federal administration responsible for consumer safety and well-being in the United States is the Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC). The CPSC is an independent agency that was created by Congress in 1972 under the Consumer Product Safety Act. The mission of the CPSC is to protect the public from unreasonable risks of injury or death associated with consumer products.

The CPSC regulates a wide range of consumer products, including toys, household appliances, electronics, furniture, clothing, and recreational products. The agency sets safety standards for these products, conducts product testing and research, investigates product-related injuries and deaths, and enforces regulations and recalls when necessary. The CPSC also provides information to consumers about product recalls, safety tips, and other consumer safety issues.

In addition to the CPSC, there are other federal agencies that also play a role in protecting consumer safety and well-being in specific areas. For example, the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) is responsible for regulating food, drugs, and cosmetics, while the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) oversees motor vehicle safety. The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) enforces consumer protection laws related to unfair or deceptive business practices, including false advertising and fraud.
