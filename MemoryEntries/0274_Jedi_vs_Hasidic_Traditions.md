Methodism is a denomination within Protestant Christianity that emphasizes practical and social holiness, along with a strong sense of community and outreach. It originated as a movement within the Church of England in the 18th century, led by John Wesley and his brother Charles Wesley. Here are some key aspects of Methodism:

### Beliefs
1. **Salvation**: Methodists believe in salvation through faith in Jesus Christ, emphasizing the importance of personal and social holiness.
2. **Grace**: Central to Methodist theology is the concept of grace, which is understood in three forms:
   - **Prevenient Grace**: God's grace that precedes human decision.
   - **Justifying Grace**: God's forgiveness and acceptance of a person as righteous.
   - **Sanctifying Grace**: The ongoing process of becoming more holy and Christ-like.
3. **Scripture**: The Bible is considered the primary source of authority, but Methodists also value tradition, reason, and experience.

### Practices
1. **Worship**: Methodist worship services typically include hymns, prayers, Scripture readings, a sermon, and sacraments.
2. **Sacraments**: Methodists observe two sacraments: Baptism and Holy Communion.
3. **Community and Social Action**: Methodism has a strong emphasis on social justice, charity, and community service.

### Organization
1. **Connectionalism**: The Methodist Church is organized in a connectional system, meaning local congregations are connected in a larger network that includes regional conferences and a global general conference.
2. **Ministry**: Both ordained ministers and laypeople play important roles in the life of the church.

### Heritage
- **John Wesley**: An Anglican clergyman, John Wesley emphasized a methodical approach to religious practice and piety, which led to the term "Methodist."
- **Charles Wesley**: Known for his hymn writing, Charles contributed significantly to Methodist worship and theology through music.

### Global Presence
Methodism has grown into a global movement, with significant presence in countries like the United States, the United Kingdom, and various parts of Africa and Asia.

Methodism is known for its inclusive approach to worship and community life, focusing on living out Christian faith through actions that improve society and support those in need.
