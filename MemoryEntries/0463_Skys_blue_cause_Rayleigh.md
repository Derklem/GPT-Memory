Why is the sky blue

**assistant:**



The sky appears blue because of a phenomenon called Rayleigh scattering. The Earth's atmosphere is composed of tiny molecules of nitrogen and oxygen, as well as other gases and particles. When sunlight enters the atmosphere, it collides with these molecules, and the light waves get scattered in all directions. 

However, blue light waves are shorter and more easily scattered than other colors of light, which is why the sky appears blue during the day. As the sun rises and sets, the light has to pass through more of the Earth's atmosphere, and the blue light waves are scattered even more, giving the sky a reddish or orange hue.

It's also worth noting that the color of the sky can change depending on factors such as the amount of moisture and pollution in the air. For example, when there are more particles in the atmosphere, such as during a sunrise or sunset, the sky can appear more orange or red.
