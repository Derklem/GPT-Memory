Determining the most credible online master's degree programs can be subjective and dependent on various factors, including your field of study, career goals, and personal preferences. However, several factors can help you evaluate the credibility of an online master's program:

1. Accreditation: Look for programs that are accredited by recognized accrediting bodies. Regional accreditation in the United States, for example, is often considered a key indicator of quality. Specific program-level accreditation in your field can also be important.

2. Reputation: Consider programs offered by well-established and reputable universities and colleges. Programs from institutions known for excellence in your chosen field are often a good choice.

3. Faculty Qualifications: Investigate the qualifications and expertise of the program's faculty members. Faculty members with strong academic and professional backgrounds can enhance the program's credibility.

4. Alumni Success: Research the career outcomes and achievements of graduates from the program. Strong alumni networks and success stories can indicate the program's effectiveness.

5. Program Rankings: While rankings should not be the sole determinant, they can provide some insights into the quality and reputation of a program. Be sure to look at rankings specific to your field of study.

6. Student Reviews and Testimonials: Seek out reviews and testimonials from current and former students to get a sense of their experiences with the program.

7. Program Curriculum: Evaluate the curriculum to ensure it aligns with your academic and career goals. Look for programs that offer the courses and specializations you need.

8. Technology and Resources: Assess the online learning platform, resources, and support services available to online students. A user-friendly platform and comprehensive support can enhance your learning experience.

9. Financial Aid and Tuition: Consider the cost of the program and the availability of financial aid options, scholarships, or discounts. Ensure that the program offers good value for your investment.

10. Flexibility: Determine if the program's schedule and format align with your work and personal commitments. Online programs should be flexible enough to accommodate your needs.

To identify credible online master's degree programs, it's essential to research and compare multiple options, reach out to program advisors, and consult with professionals in your desired field for recommendations. Ultimately, the credibility of a program is closely tied to your specific goals and how well a program aligns with them.

Determining the cheapest online master's degree programs can be complex, as costs can vary significantly based on factors such as residency, program length, and the specific degree pursued. Additionally, many universities adjust their tuition fees periodically. As of my last update in September 2021, I don't have access to real-time data on current tuition fees. However, I can offer some general insights into finding affordable options:

1. **Public Universities:** In-state tuition at public universities in your own state or country is often considerably cheaper than out-of-state or international tuition. If you're considering a university in your state, the in-state tuition rates are generally more affordable.

2. **Online-Specific Universities:** Some universities, such as Southern New Hampshire University and Western Governors University, are known for their online degree programs and offer competitive tuition rates.

3. **Financial Aid and Scholarships:** Many universities offer financial aid and scholarships to online students. These can significantly reduce the overall cost of your education. Be sure to research the scholarships available specifically for online students.

4. **Flat-Rate Tuition:** Some universities offer flat-rate tuition for online students, allowing them to take a higher course load for a fixed fee. This can be cost-effective if you plan to take a lot of classes.

5. **Credit Transfers:** If you have completed previous coursework, some universities allow you to transfer credits, reducing the number of courses you need to take and, consequently, the overall cost.

6. **Community Colleges:** While community colleges traditionally offer associate degrees, some are now offering online bachelor's degree completion programs in partnership with four-year institutions. These programs can be more affordable than traditional university programs.

7. **MOOC-Based Degrees:** Some universities offer master's degrees through Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) on platforms like Coursera and edX. These can be more affordable than traditional degrees, although they often come with a fee for the verified certificate and assessment.

When researching online master's programs, it's important to consider the total cost, including tuition, fees, textbooks, and any other expenses. Also, consider the long-term benefits of the degree in relation to its cost. Remember to check with the specific institutions you are interested in for the most current and detailed financial information.
