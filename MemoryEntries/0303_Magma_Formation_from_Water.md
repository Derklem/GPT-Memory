The connection between deep Earth water and magma lies in the Earth's mantle, where water plays a crucial role in the melting process. As tectonic plates move and subduct, water-rich minerals are dragged into the mantle. This water lowers the melting point of the mantle rocks, leading to magma formation. In regions where subduction is occurring, such as deep ocean trenches, water facilitates the melting of the mantle, creating magma chambers and leading to volcanic activity. Conversely, as we burrow deeper on land, we may encounter groundwater due to percolation, but the presence of magma depends on tectonic activity and the geological context of the region.
