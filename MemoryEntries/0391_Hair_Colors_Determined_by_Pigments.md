The color of your hair is determined by the amount and type of pigments in your hair follicles. The two main types of pigments that determine hair color are eumelanin, which produces brown or black hair, and pheomelanin, which produces red or blonde hair.

As we age, our bodies produce less of these pigments, which can lead to gray hair. Genetics also play a role in determining the color of your hair, and it is common to have multiple shades of hair on your head.

Additionally, environmental factors such as exposure to sunlight, chemicals in hair products, and certain medications can also affect the color and health of your hair.

Overall, the combination of genetics, age, and environmental factors can result in the different colors of hair on your head.
