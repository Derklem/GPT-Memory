# Master's Options for BAs

_Created: 2023-09-28T19:45:14+00:00_

**user:**

if i have a bachelor of arts degree what masters degree can i get

**assistant:**

Having a Bachelor of Arts (BA) degree provides you with a wide range of options for pursuing a Master's degree. The specific Master's program you can pursue will depend on various factors, including your interests, career goals, and the specific field of study you wish to specialize in. Here are some Master's degree options that are often available to BA degree holders:

1. Master of Arts (MA): This is a natural progression from a Bachelor of Arts degree. MA programs are available in various fields such as English, history, psychology, sociology, political science, and more. They are typically research-oriented and can be a good choice if you want to deepen your knowledge in your undergraduate major.

2. Master of Fine Arts (MFA): If you have a BA in a creative field like art, writing, or performing arts, you can pursue an MFA to further develop your artistic skills and create a portfolio of work.

3. Master of Business Administration (MBA): If you want to transition into the business world, an MBA can be a valuable choice. Many MBA programs do not require a specific undergraduate degree, so BA holders are often eligible to apply.

4. Master of Science (MS): Depending on your undergraduate major, you can pursue an MS in a related field. For example, if you have a BA in biology, you can pursue an MS in biology, environmental science, or a related discipline.

5. Master of Public Administration (MPA): An MPA is a suitable choice if you're interested in working in government, public policy, or nonprofit organizations.

6. Master of Social Work (MSW): If you're interested in social work and counseling, you can pursue an MSW to become a licensed social worker.

7. Master of Education (MEd) or Master of Arts in Teaching (MAT): If you want to become a teacher or work in education, these degrees are designed to prepare educators for teaching roles.

8. Master of Public Health (MPH): If you're interested in public health and healthcare administration, an MPH can be a suitable choice.

9. Master of International Relations (MIR) or Master of International Affairs (MIA): These programs are for those interested in international relations, diplomacy, and global affairs.

10. Master of Library and Information Science (MLIS): If you have a passion for libraries and information management, an MLIS can lead to a career as a librarian or information professional.

Remember that the specific prerequisites and admission requirements for Master's programs can vary by institution and program. It's important to research programs of interest and contact the admissions offices for detailed information on eligibility and application requirements. Your choice of Master's degree should align with your career goals and personal interests to ensure a fulfilling academic and professional journey.
